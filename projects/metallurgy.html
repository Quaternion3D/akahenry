Metallurgy

Around the same time, I also had an interest in working with metal. I modified an old roast pan to create a small forge which was capable of melting aluminum cans. My first designs used a charcoal fire supercharged by forcing air into it with a mattress pump. Later, I created a forge with firebricks to contain the heat better, and used a propane burner that I built which was easier and faster to light than charcoal. After I melted enough cans to fill the crucible, I would pour the molten aluminum into muffin tins to form ingots. I also used the forge to heat up rail road spikes (made of low carbon steel), which I hammered into rudimentary blades just for fun.
